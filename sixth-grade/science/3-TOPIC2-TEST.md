### Section F: Cell Transport

**46.** Passive transport moves substances from HIGH concentration to LOW concentration and does NOT require energy. Active transport moves substances from LOW concentration to HIGH concentration (against the flow) and DOES require energy (ATP).

**47.** Two examples of substances that move by diffusion: **CO2 (carbon dioxide)** and **O2 (oxygen)**

**48.** Osmosis is the diffusion of water molecules through a semipermeable membrane. Water moves from an area where there is MORE water (high water concentration) to an area where there is LESS water (low water concentration).

**49.** Facilitated diffusion is a type of passive transport where proteins in the cell membrane form channels to help substances cross. Some substances, like sugar, cannot easily pass through the membrane on their own, so they need these protein channels to get through.

**50.** Endocytosis is when a cell takes IN large particles by engulfing them. Exocytosis is when a cell pushes large particles OUT. Both are examples of active transport because they require energy (ATP) and move materials against the normal flow.

### Section I: Short Answer / Essay (7 points)

**35.** Sample answer (award up to 4 points):

Both parents must have the **genotype** Bb -- they are **hybrid** for the eye color gene. Brown (B) is the **dominant allele**, so even though both parents carry one copy of the blue-eye allele (b), it is "hidden" because the dominant B allele masks it. Their **phenotype** is brown eyes, but they each carry a recessive b allele. When we cross Bb x Bb in a **Punnett square**, we get BB, Bb, Bb, and bb. The bb combination means the child got one recessive allele from each parent, and with no dominant allele to mask it, the recessive blue-eye trait is expressed. There is a **25% probability** this will happen.

**Scoring rubric:**
- (1 pt) Correctly identifies both parents' genotypes as Bb
- (1 pt) Explains or draws the Punnett square correctly
- (1 pt) Explains why blue eyes can be hidden (recessive allele masked by dominant)
- (1 pt) Uses at least two vocabulary words correctly (e.g., genotype, phenotype, dominant, recessive, allele, hybrid, Punnett square, probability)

**36.** Sample answer (award up to 3 points):

**Mitosis** is cell division used for growth and repair. It produces **2 identical daughter cells**, each with a **full set of DNA**. Your body uses mitosis when you grow taller or when your skin heals after a cut. **Meiosis** is cell division used for sexual reproduction. It produces **4 cells**, each with **half the genetic information** (one copy of each gene). Your body uses meiosis to create egg cells (in females) or sperm cells (in males). During **fertilization**, the egg and sperm combine to restore the full set of DNA in the new organism.

**Scoring rubric:**
- (1 pt) Correctly states the purpose of each (mitosis = growth/repair; meiosis = making reproductive cells)
- (1 pt) Correctly describes the number of cells produced and DNA amount (mitosis = 2 identical, full DNA; meiosis = 4 cells, half DNA)
- (1 pt) Gives a real-life example for each type

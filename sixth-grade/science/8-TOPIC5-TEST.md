### Section B: Short Answer / Essay (6 points)

**7.** Sample answer (award up to 6 points):

A **conventional oven** cooks food mainly using **radiation** -- the heating elements radiate heat toward the food, but the hot air just sits around without moving much. A **convection oven** has a built-in fan that blows the hot air around the inside of the oven, creating **convection currents**. Because the hot air is actively moving and circulating around the food, **heat transfer** happens more evenly and more efficiently. This means the food cooks faster, which is why you can lower the temperature by 25 degrees and still get the same results. Without the fan, some spots in a conventional oven end up hotter than others, so food can cook unevenly.

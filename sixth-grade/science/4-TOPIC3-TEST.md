### Section J: Short Answer (Questions 56-60)

**56.** The four steps of the stimulus-response cycle for being too hot: (1) You sense that your body is too hot (stimulus). (2) You respond by seeking a cooler temperature, such as going inside or finding shade. (3) You sense that your body temperature is back to normal. (4) You resume your normal activity. This cycle is an example of how the body maintains homeostasis.

**57.** The somatic nervous system controls voluntary actions -- things you choose to do, like raising your hand or kicking a ball. The autonomic nervous system controls involuntary actions -- things that happen automatically without you thinking about them, like your heartbeat, breathing, or digestion.

**58.** In the double loop, the first loop sends blood from the heart to the lungs, where it picks up oxygen and drops off carbon dioxide. The blood then returns to the heart. In the second loop, the heart pumps the oxygen-rich blood out to the rest of the body to deliver oxygen to cells, and the blood picks up carbon dioxide and waste before returning to the heart.

**59.** Negative feedback is the body's way of maintaining homeostasis by reversing a change when something gets too high or too low. For example, when your body temperature gets too high, the body responds by sweating to cool you down. Once your temperature returns to normal, the sweating stops. The body senses the change, corrects it, and then stops correcting once balance is restored.

**60.** The six types of nutrients are: carbohydrates, proteins, fats, vitamins, minerals, and water. (Student should explain any three. Examples below.)
- **Carbohydrates** are the body's primary source of fuel and energy. They come in simple and complex forms.
- **Proteins** help with growth and repair of body tissues.
- **Fats** store energy for later use, help make hormones, and support nerve function.
- **Vitamins** help the body perform important chemical processes.
- **Minerals** help build strong bones and teeth and support other body functions.
- **Water** is needed for nearly every body process and makes up a large part of the body.

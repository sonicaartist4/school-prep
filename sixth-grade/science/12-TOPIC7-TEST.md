### Section B: Short Answer/Essay with Graph -- Solar Energy

**2a)** As average global temperature increases over time, the percentage of solar energy reflected by Earth's surface **decreases**. There is an inverse relationship -- the warmer it gets, the less reflective Earth's surface becomes. **(2 points: 1 pt for identifying the decreasing trend, 1 pt for connecting it to temperature increase)**

**2b)** As temperatures rise, **ice and snow melt**. Ice and snow are bright white surfaces that reflect a lot of solar energy back into space. When they melt, they are replaced by darker surfaces like ocean water or bare ground, which **absorb more energy** instead of reflecting it. This means less energy is reflected and more is absorbed, which can cause even more warming. **(2 points: 1 pt for mentioning ice/snow melting, 1 pt for explaining that darker surfaces absorb more and reflect less)**

### Section H: Short Answer/Essay -- Density and Sea Breezes

**13.** During the day, the sun heats the land faster than the ocean. The hot land heats the air above it, making that air **less dense** (warm air is less dense because the molecules spread apart). Because the warm air is less dense, it **rises**, creating an area of **low pressure** over the land. Meanwhile, the air over the ocean is **cooler and more dense**, creating an area of **high pressure** over the water. Wind always flows from high pressure to low pressure, so the cooler, denser air over the ocean flows toward the land to replace the rising warm air. That's the sea breeze!

**(4 points: 1 pt for explaining warm air is less dense and rises, 1 pt for explaining cool air is more dense, 1 pt for identifying low pressure over land / high pressure over ocean, 1 pt for explaining air flows from high to low pressure)**

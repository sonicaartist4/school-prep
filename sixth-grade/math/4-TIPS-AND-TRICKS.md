# Saxon Math Course 3 - Tips and Tricks

> **Quick shortcuts, memory tricks, and clever strategies to help you work faster and make fewer mistakes!**

---

## GENERAL TEST-TAKING TIPS

### Before You Start
- **Write down key formulas** on scratch paper as soon as the test begins (while they're fresh in your head)
- **Skim the whole test first** — do the easy problems first to build confidence and save time for hard ones
- **Budget your time** — if you have 50 questions and 90 minutes, that's less than 2 minutes per question

### While Working
- **Read each problem TWICE** before picking up your pencil
- **Circle key words** — "difference" means subtract, "product" means multiply, "quotient" means divide, "sum" means add
- **Show ALL your work** — even if you can do it in your head, write it down for partial credit
- **Estimate first** — before solving, guess roughly what the answer should be so you can catch big mistakes
- **Don't leave anything blank** — even a guess or partial work is better than nothing

### Before Turning It In
- **Plug your answer back in** to check if it works
- **Make sure units match** — if the question asks for feet, don't give inches
- **Recheck negative signs** — the #1 source of careless mistakes

## NUMBER TRICKS

### Multiplying by 5
Multiply by 10 and then divide by 2 (it's way easier).
- 24 × 5 = 24 × 10 ÷ 2 = 240 ÷ 2 = **120**
- 86 × 5 = 860 ÷ 2 = **430**

### Multiplying by 9
Multiply by 10 and subtract the original number.
- 7 × 9 = 7 × 10 - 7 = 70 - 7 = **63**
- 15 × 9 = 150 - 15 = **135**

### Multiplying by 11
For two-digit numbers: put the sum of the digits in the middle.
- 36 × 11: 3_(3+6)_6 = **396**
- 45 × 11: 4_(4+5)_5 = **495**
- If the middle sum is more than 9, carry the 1: 78 × 11 → 7_(15)_8 → **858**

### Squaring Numbers Ending in 5
Take the tens digit, multiply it by one more than itself, then stick 25 on the end.
- 35² → 3 × 4 = 12, stick on 25 → **1,225**
- 65² → 6 × 7 = 42, stick on 25 → **4,225**
- 85² → 8 × 9 = 72, stick on 25 → **7,225**

### The 9s Finger Trick (for the 9 times table)
Hold up all 10 fingers. For 9 × N, put down finger #N from the left.
- 9 × 3: put down finger 3 → 2 fingers on the left, 7 on the right → **27**
- 9 × 7: put down finger 7 → 6 on left, 3 on right → **63**

### Checking if a Number is Divisible
- **By 2:** Last digit is even (0, 2, 4, 6, 8)
- **By 3:** Add all the digits — if the sum is divisible by 3, the number is too (e.g., 123 → 1+2+3 = 6 → yes!)
- **By 4:** The last two digits form a number divisible by 4 (e.g., 316 → 16 ÷ 4 = 4 → yes!)
- **By 5:** Last digit is 0 or 5
- **By 6:** Divisible by BOTH 2 and 3
- **By 9:** Add all the digits — if the sum is divisible by 9, the number is too (e.g., 738 → 7+3+8 = 18 → yes!)
- **By 10:** Last digit is 0

## FRACTION TRICKS

### Keep-Change-Flip (Dividing Fractions)
Never forget this one! When dividing fractions:
1. **Keep** the first fraction the same
2. **Change** the division sign to multiplication
3. **Flip** the second fraction upside down

Example: 3/4 ÷ 2/5 → **Keep** 3/4, **Change** to ×, **Flip** to 5/2 → 3/4 × 5/2 = 15/8

### Butterfly Method (Comparing or Adding Fractions)
To compare 3/7 and 5/9, cross-multiply up:
- 3 × 9 = 27 (write above 3/7)
- 5 × 7 = 35 (write above 5/9)
- 27 < 35, so 3/7 < 5/9

This also works for adding! The cross-products become the new numerators, and multiply the denominators for the new bottom.

### Mixed Number to Improper Fraction — "MAD" Method
**M**ultiply the whole number by the denominator, **A**dd the numerator, put over the **D**enominator.
- 3 2/5 → (3 × 5 + 2) / 5 = **17/5**
- 7 1/3 → (7 × 3 + 1) / 3 = **22/3**

### Quick Simplifying
Before multiplying fractions, cross-cancel diagonally to keep numbers small.
- 4/9 × 3/8 → cancel 4 and 8 (both ÷ 4), cancel 3 and 9 (both ÷ 3) → 1/3 × 1/2 = **1/6**

## PERCENT TRICKS

### Finding 10% (The Starting Point for Everything)
Just move the decimal one place to the left!
- 10% of 250 = **25**
- 10% of 83 = **8.3**

### Building Other Percents from 10%
- **5%** = half of 10% → 5% of 250 = 25 ÷ 2 = 12.50
- **20%** = double 10% → 20% of 250 = 25 × 2 = 50
- **15%** = 10% + 5% → 15% of 250 = 25 + 12.50 = 37.50
- **25%** = divide by 4 → 25% of 250 = 250 ÷ 4 = 62.50
- **50%** = divide by 2 → 50% of 250 = 125
- **1%** = move decimal two places left → 1% of 250 = 2.50

### Restaurant Tip Trick
Need to calculate a 15% tip? Find 10% (move the decimal), then add half of that.
- Bill = $48 → 10% = $4.80, half = $2.40 → 15% tip = **$7.20**

### "Is/Of" Trick for Percent Word Problems
- "What **is** 30% **of** 80?" → the word "is" = equals, "of" = multiply
- Becomes: x = 0.30 × 80 = 24

## ALGEBRA TRICKS

### Solving Equations — "Undo" in Reverse Order
Whatever was done LAST to x, undo it FIRST.
- 3x + 5 = 20 → addition was last, so subtract 5 first → 3x = 15 → then divide by 3 → x = 5

### Checking Your Answer
ALWAYS plug your answer back into the original equation.
- Solved x = 5 for 3x + 5 = 20? Check: 3(5) + 5 = 15 + 5 = 20 ✓

### Sign Change When Crossing the Equals Sign
When you move a term to the other side of an equation, its sign flips.
- x + 3 = 10 → x = 10 - 3 (the +3 became -3)
- x - 7 = 4 → x = 4 + 7 (the -7 became +7)

### Distributing Negative Signs
Be extra careful when there's a negative outside the parentheses — EVERY sign inside flips!
- -(3x - 5) = **-3x + 5** (not -3x - 5!)
- -2(x + 4) = **-2x - 8**

### The "Magic" of FOIL
**F**irst, **O**uter, **I**nner, **L**ast — and the middle terms usually combine.
- (x + 3)(x + 2) = x² + 2x + 3x + 6 = x² + **5x** + 6
- The "5" is just 3 + 2, and the "6" is 3 × 2!

## GEOMETRY TRICKS

### Remembering Complementary vs. Supplementary
- **C**omplementary = **C**orner = 90° (a right angle, like the corner of a paper)
- **S**upplementary = **S**traight = 180° (a straight line)

### Area of a Triangle — "Half a Rectangle"
A triangle is literally half of a rectangle! That's why the formula is ½ × base × height.
- If a rectangle is 10 × 6 = 60, the triangle inside it = 60 ÷ 2 = **30**

### Circumference Shortcut
C = πd — just multiply the diameter by about 3.14.
- Diameter = 20 → C ≈ 20 × 3.14 = **62.8**

### Volume Memory Trick
- **Prisms and cylinders:** V = Base Area × height (full stack)
- **Pyramids and cones:** V = ⅓ × Base Area × height (one-third of the stack)
- **Sphere:** V = 4/3 × π × r³ (the weird one — just memorize it!)

### Pythagorean Triples to Know
Memorize these so you don't have to calculate every time:
- **3, 4, 5** (and 6-8-10, 9-12-15, 12-16-20, 15-20-25)
- **5, 12, 13** (and 10-24-26)
- **8, 15, 17**
- **7, 24, 25**

If you see legs of 9 and 12, you should instantly know the hypotenuse is 15 (it's 3-4-5 tripled!).

### SOH-CAH-TOA Story
**S**ome **O**ld **H**orse **C**aught **A** **H**orse **T**aking **O**ats **A**way
- **S**in = **O**pposite / **H**ypotenuse
- **C**os = **A**djacent / **H**ypotenuse
- **T**an = **O**pposite / **A**djacent

## INTEGER TRICKS

### Adding Integers — The Money Way
- Positive = money you HAVE
- Negative = money you OWE
- (+7) + (-3) = you have $7 but owe $3 = **$4 left**

### Subtracting Integers — "Add the Opposite"
Change every subtraction to addition of the opposite, then use addition rules.
- 5 - (-3) = 5 + 3 = **8** (taking away a debt is like getting money!)
- -4 - 6 = -4 + (-6) = **-10**

### Multiplying/Dividing Sign Rules
Just count the negative signs:
- **Even** number of negatives → **positive** answer
- **Odd** number of negatives → **negative** answer
- (-2) × (-3) × (-4) → three negatives (odd) → **-24**
- (-1) × (-1) × (-2) × (-3) → four negatives (even) → **+6**

## EXPONENT & SCIENTIFIC NOTATION TRICKS

### Negative Exponents Aren't Negative Numbers!
A negative exponent just means "flip it." It doesn't make the answer negative.
- 2⁻³ = 1/2³ = 1/8 = **0.125** (positive!)
- 5⁻¹ = 1/5 = **0.2** (also positive!)

### Scientific Notation — Counting Decimal Moves
- Big number → positive exponent (count how many places you moved the decimal LEFT)
  - 45,000,000 → 4.5 × 10⁷ (moved 7 places left)
- Small number → negative exponent (count how many places you moved the decimal RIGHT)
  - 0.00032 → 3.2 × 10⁻⁴ (moved 4 places right)

### Simplifying Square Roots — Find the Biggest Perfect Square
Always look for the LARGEST perfect square factor:
- √72: think... is 4 a factor? Yes (72 = 4 × 18). But is 36 a factor? Yes! (72 = 36 × 2)
- Use 36: √72 = √36 × √2 = **6√2** (one step instead of two!)

## WORD PROBLEM STRATEGIES

### Key Word Decoder
| Word/Phrase | Operation |
|---|---|
| sum, total, combined, together, increased by | **Addition (+)** |
| difference, less than, decreased by, fewer, remaining | **Subtraction (-)** |
| product, times, of, each, every | **Multiplication (×)** |
| quotient, per, divided, split, shared equally | **Division (÷)** |
| is, equals, was, gives | **= (equals sign)** |

### The "Let x = ..." Strategy
When a word problem feels confusing, write "Let x = ..." and define what you're looking for. Then build the equation from the words.

**Example:** "Five more than twice a number is 17."
- Let x = the number
- Twice a number = 2x
- Five more than that = 2x + 5
- Is 17 = equals 17
- Equation: 2x + 5 = 17 → x = **6**

### Draw It Out
For geometry and measurement problems, ALWAYS sketch a picture. Label every number from the problem. Often, just seeing it makes the solution obvious.

### Work Backwards (for Multiple Choice)
If you're stuck, plug in each answer choice and see which one makes the equation true.

**Example:** Which value of x makes 3x - 7 = 14 true? Options: A) 5, B) 7, C) 3
- A: 3(5) - 7 = 8 ✗
- B: 3(7) - 7 = 14 ✓ — that's it!

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Forgetting to flip the inequality sign** when multiplying/dividing by a negative
2. **Not distributing the negative** to ALL terms: -(2x - 3) = -2x **+ 3**, NOT -2x - 3
3. **Order of operations mistakes:** 2 + 3 × 4 = **14**, not 20
4. **Mixing up area and perimeter** — area is inside (square units), perimeter is around (regular units)
5. **Forgetting ± when taking square roots:** x² = 25 means x = 5 OR x = -5
6. **Dividing by zero** — you can't do it! If zero is in the denominator, the answer is undefined
7. **Not simplifying fractions** — always reduce to lowest terms
8. **Confusing radius and diameter** — diameter is TWICE the radius (d = 2r)
9. **Forgetting to convert units** — especially in area (square) and volume (cubic) problems
10. **Rushing through negative signs** — slow down and double-check every negative

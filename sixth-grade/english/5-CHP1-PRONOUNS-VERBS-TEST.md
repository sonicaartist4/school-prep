### Section N: Short Answer

**95.** Substitute "he" or "him" into the sentence. If "he" sounds right, use **who**. If "him" sounds right, use **whom**. (HIM and WHOM both end in M.)

**96.** An action verb tells what the subject DOES (e.g., "She **runs**"). A linking verb tells what the subject IS — it links the subject to a description (e.g., "She **is** tall"). If you can replace the verb with "am/is/are" and the sentence still makes sense, it's a linking verb.

**97.** "Hisself" and "theirselves" aren't real words in standard English — they're common mistakes. Use **himself** (instead of hisself) and **themselves** (instead of theirselves).

**98.** Possessive pronouns ALREADY show ownership on their own — adding an apostrophe is unnecessary and actually wrong. Apostrophes are used for contractions (like "it's" = "it is"), not for possessive pronouns like "its," "hers," or "theirs."

**99.** A verb phrase is a main verb plus one or more helping verbs (like "has eaten" or "will be going"). When words like "not," "never," "always," "hardly," or "surely" come in the middle, those words are NOT part of the verb phrase — just pull them out and the verb phrase is what's left.

**100.** The five interrogative pronouns are **who, whom, whose, what, and which**.
